### 3 - Evaluation of Common Denormalization Techniques
* **Collapsing Relations (Highly Recommended):** Since `demography`, `employment`, `education`, `economy`, and `health` share the same grain (`country_code` + `year`), they should be collapsed into a single table. `region` should also be collapsed into `country`.

We can do the same for `population` (by age group/gender) and `life_expectancy` (by gender).
* **Vertical Partitioning (Recommended):** Instead of creating One Big Table (OBT) for absolutely everything, we should keep "Yearly Macro Indicators" and "Poverty Surveys" as two separate wide tables. Surveys do not happen every year for every country; forcing them into the yearly indicators table would result in massive amounts of `NULL` values and complex analytical handling.

### 4 - Design Multiple Possible Denormalized Schemas

### 5 - Evaluate and Choose the Best Schema
**Schema Option C** is the absolute best choice for this specific analytical workload.

Pivoting the child tables eliminates expensive `GROUP BY` and `SUM()` operations.
