### 3. Evaluation of Denormalization Techniques

*   **Adding Redundant Attributes:** Best for `Employee` (Salary, Hours) and `PayHistory` (Rates). We will move these to a dedicated table.
*   **Adding Derived Attributes:** Best for `SalesTerritory` (SalesYTD, CostYTD) and `WorkOrderRouting` (Planned vs Actual).
*   **Collapsing Relations:** Good for `Person` (Address, Contact, Phone, Email) and `Store` (Demographics). We will consolidate these into a single `Person` or `Store` table with a composite key.
*   **Vertical Partitioning:** Not strictly necessary for the main business logic, but could be used for `Person` (splitting into `Person` and `PersonContact` if XML is heavy). Given the schema complexity, consolidating into a single `Person` table is more efficient for read performance than splitting.
