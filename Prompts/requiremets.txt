<Requirements>
    To ensure that the denormalized version is accurately represented, two important parameters must be considered:
    Completeness and Correctness.

    Completeness: This is defined as the ratio between the number of attributes in the normalized model and the number of
                attributes in the denormalized model.
                The ideal value is 1, meaning that no information was added or lost during the transformation.
                    If the ratio is greater than 1, it indicates that the normalized model contains more attributes, meaning that some
                information was lost in the transition.
                    If the ratio is less than 1, it indicates that the denormalized model contains more attributes, meaning that new
                information was added in the transition.

    Correctness: To assess correctness, the following aspects must be verified:
        1 - The correct data types of all attributes were preserved,
        2 - The semantics of identifiers were maintained,
        3 - The cardinality of relationships was preserved.
</Requirements>
