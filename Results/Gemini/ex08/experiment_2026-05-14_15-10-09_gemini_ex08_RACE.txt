### 1 - Deep Analysis of the Provided SQL Script
The normalized model consists of 10 tables representing three distinct domains:
1. **Student & Academic Domain:** `aluno_ifb`, `curso_ifb`, `matricula_ifb`.
2. **Professional/Employment Domain:** `vinculo_empregaticio`, `remuneracao`, `localizacao` (for job location).
3. **School Context Domain:** `escola`, `localizacao` (for school location), `desempenho_enem`, `indicadores_escolares`, `participacao_enem`.

**Current Bottlenecks:** 
- The model is highly normalized (3NF). 
- `matricula_ifb` and `vinculo_empregaticio` act as separate transaction/event tables, both pointing to `aluno_ifb`.
- School metrics are split across 4 different tables (`escola`, `desempenho`, `indicadores`, `participacao`), requiring massive JOINs just to get a unified view of a school's performance in a given year.

### 2 - Frequent Queries / Workload Context
In an analytical, read-heavy workload (OLAP) for an educational institution, the most frequent queries will likely be:
- *“What is the average salary (`remuneracao`) of students who graduated from courses (`curso_ifb`) in schools with high ENEM scores (`desempenho_enem`)?”*
- *“How does the school dropout rate (`indicadores_escolares`) correlate with the student's employment status (`vinculo_empregaticio`)?”*

**JOIN Bottleneck:** Answering the first query currently requires joining **8 tables**: `remuneracao` $\rightarrow$ `vinculo_empregaticio` $\rightarrow$ `aluno_ifb` $\rightarrow$ `matricula_ifb` $\rightarrow$ `curso_ifb` $\rightarrow$ `escola` $\rightarrow$ `desempenho_enem` $\rightarrow$ `localizacao`. This will severely degrade performance on large datasets.

### 3 - Evaluate Common Denormalization Techniques
- **1 - Adding Redundant Attributes:** Highly applicable. We should bring `uf` and `municipio` directly into the school and employment tables to eliminate the `localizacao` table entirely.
- **2 - Adding Derived Attributes:** We can pre-calculate some flags, but the primary focus should be on flattening the structure.
- **3 - Collapsing Relations:** **Crucial here.** We can collapse `remuneracao` directly into `vinculo_empregaticio`. We can also collapse all school indicator tables into a single yearly school dimension.
- **4 - Vertical Partitioning:** Not strictly necessary, but we *must* logically partition the fact tables to avoid the Cartesian product mentioned earlier. Academic facts and Employment facts should remain separate but share dimensions.

### 4 - Design Multiple Possible Schemas
**Option A: One Big Table (OBT)**
- Everything is merged into a single table. 
- *Pros:* Zero JOINs.
- *Cons:* Massive data duplication. If a student has 3 enrollments and 4 jobs, their data is duplicated 12 times. Aggregating salaries or enrollments becomes extremely difficult without complex `COUNT(DISTINCT)` logic.

**Option B: The Single Trajectory Fact (Prompt's Draft)**
- `fato_aluno_trajetoria` + `dim_escola_indicadores_anuais`.
- *Pros:* Reduces tables to 2.
- *Cons:* Suffers from the same Cartesian product risk as Option A regarding enrollments vs. jobs.

**Option C: Multi-Fact Dimensional Model (Conformed Star Schema)**
- `dim_escola_anual`: Collapses all school metrics and locations.
- `fato_matricula_enriquecida`: Collapses student, course, and enrollment data.
- `fato_emprego_enriquecido`: Collapses employment, remuneration, and location data.
- *Pros:* Eliminates the Cartesian product risk. Reduces the 10-table model to just 3 highly performant tables. Queries only require a maximum of 1 or 2 JOINs.

### 5 - Evaluate and Choose the Best Schema
**Option C** is the absolute best choice for a Senior Data Engineering architecture. It perfectly balances the performance gains of denormalization (reducing 8-table joins to 1-table or 2-table joins) while maintaining data integrity and accurate aggregations. 

We will create:
1. **`dim_escola_anual`**: A dimension table that acts as a snapshot of a school's infrastructure and performance per year.
2. **`fato_matricula_academica`**: A fact table containing everything about the student, their enrollment, and the course.
3. **`fato_vinculo_profissional`**: A fact table containing everything about the student's job, salary, and company location.

### Why this is the superior model:
1. **No Cartesian Products:** By keeping `fato_matricula_academica` and `fato_vinculo_profissional` separate, calculating the average salary of a student doesn't accidentally multiply by the number of courses they took.
2. **Massive I/O Reduction:** A query analyzing the impact of school infrastructure on student salaries now only requires joining `fato_vinculo_profissional` $\rightarrow$ `fato_matricula_academica` $\rightarrow$ `dim_escola_anual` (2 JOINs instead of 8).
3. **Columnar/Analytical Friendly:** This wide-table format is highly optimized for modern columnar OLAP databases (like Redshift, BigQuery, or PostgreSQL with columnar extensions), where wide tables with redundant string attributes compress exceptionally well.
