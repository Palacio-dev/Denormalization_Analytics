Here is the workload-driven denormalization strategy and the resulting physical schema, optimized for PostgreSQL.

### 1. Workload-Driven Analysis

Based on the schema (the classic "Chinook" digital media store), a read-heavy workload will typically consist of these high-frequency queries:

1.  **Storefront Catalog Browsing:** Users searching and filtering tracks by Genre, Artist, or Album.
    *   *Current Cost:* High. Displaying a simple list of tracks requires a 5-table join (`Track` → `Album` → `Artist`, `Track` → `Genre`, `Track` → `MediaType`).
2.  **Customer Order History:** Users viewing their past invoices and the specific tracks they bought.
    *   *Current Cost:* Medium-High. Requires joining `Invoice` → `InvoiceLine` → `Track` → `Album` → `Artist`.
    *   *Correctness Risk:* If an artist changes their name or a track is renamed/re-priced, historical invoices will dynamically change, which violates accounting correctness.
3.  **Playlist Rendering:** Loading a playlist to display all its tracks.
    *   *Current Cost:* High. Requires joining `Playlist` → `PlaylistTrack` → `Track` → `Album` → `Artist`.
4.  **Customer Dashboards & Analytics:** Admin views sorting customers by total spend or viewing their assigned support rep.
    *   *Current Cost:* High. Requires on-the-fly aggregation of `Invoice` totals and joining `Customer` → `Employee`.

### 2. Denormalization Design

To achieve lower latency and preserve business correctness, we will apply the following denormalization techniques:

*   **Pre-joined "Wide" Table (`Track`):** We will flatten the catalog. We will duplicate `AlbumTitle`, `ArtistId`, `ArtistName`, `GenreName`, and `MediaTypeName` directly into the `Track` table. This eliminates 4 joins for the most frequent read operation (catalog browsing).
*   **"Snapshot" Fact Table (`InvoiceLine`):** We will copy `TrackName`, `ArtistName`, and `AlbumTitle` into `InvoiceLine` at the time of purchase. This removes the need to join to the catalog to render a receipt, and **preserves correctness** (historical receipts won't change if the catalog is updated). We will also add a precomputed `LineTotal`.
*   **Redundant Attributes & Precomputed Aggregates (`Customer`):** We will add `SupportRepName` to avoid the `Employee` join. We will also add a `LifetimeValue` column (updated via triggers/batch in a real system) to allow instant sorting of high-value customers without aggregating invoices.
*   **Star-like Read Model / Materialized View (`Playlist_Read_Model`):** Playlists are read frequently but updated infrequently. We will create a Materialized View that pre-joins playlists to the wide track table.
*   **Covering Indexes:** We will use PostgreSQL's `INCLUDE` clause to create covering indexes. When a user filters by `GenreName`, the index will contain the `TrackName` and `ArtistName`, allowing an Index-Only Scan.
