### 3 - Evaluation of Common Denormalization Techniques
*   **Collapsing Relations (Highly Recommended)**: The 9 tables can be collapsed into a One Big Table (OBT) or a few wide tables. This eliminates the 6-level JOIN bottleneck entirely.
*   **Adding Redundant Attributes (Recommended)**: Bringing `sigla_uf`, `nome_municipio`, `nome_ies`, and `nome_curso` directly into the assessment records will allow filtering and grouping without touching dimension tables.
*   **Vertical Partitioning (Recommended)**: Because `ESCOLA` is isolated from the `ANO`/`CURSO` assessment flow, joining it into a single wide table would create a Cartesian product (multiplying every course by every school in a municipality). We should partition the analytical model into two wide tables: one for Course Assessments and one for School Registrations.
*   **Adding Derived Attributes (Optional)**: The `MUNICIPIO` table already contains derived attributes (`media_ideb`, `media_saeb`, etc.). We will retain these in the denormalized model.

### 4 - Design Multiple Possible Schemas
*   **Design A (Star Schema)**:

    *   *Dimensions*: `Dim_Localidade` (UF + Municipio), `Dim_Instituicao` (IES + Curso), `Dim_Escola`.
    *   *Pros*: Reduces tables from 9 to 4. *Cons*: Still requires JOINs for standard BI reporting.
*   **Design B (Single "One Big Table" - OBT)**: 
    *   Collapse all 9 tables into a single table. 
    *   *Pros*: Zero JOINs. *Cons*: Forces a Cartesian product between `CURSO` and `ESCOLA` since they share a municipality but no direct relationship, leading to massive data duplication and skewed aggregations.
*   **Design C (Two Wide Tables - Vertical Partitioning + Collapsing)**:

    *   Table 2: `Denorm_Cadastro_Escola` (Flattens UF, Municipio, Escola).
    *   *Pros*: Zero JOINs for analytical queries. Solves the Cartesian product issue. Perfectly aligns with modern columnar database architectures.

### 5 - Evaluate and Choose the Best Schema
**Design C** is the most consistent and performant choice. It applies *Collapsing Relations* to eliminate the deep hierarchy and *Vertical Partitioning* to separate the basic education school registry from the higher education course assessment facts. This ensures that BI tools can query course performance and school distributions instantly without complex JOINs or inaccurate aggregations caused by Cartesian products.
