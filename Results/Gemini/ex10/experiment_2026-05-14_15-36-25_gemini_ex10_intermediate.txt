### 1. Workload-Driven Analysis

**The Bottleneck (High-Cost Operations):**
In your current normalized model, answering a standard analytical business question like *"What were the per-capita emissions and solar energy usage for European countries in 2022?"* requires a massive **6-way join**. 
1. The database must join `paises` to `continente` to filter by Europe.
2. It must join `paises` to `populacao` (on country ID and year).
3. It must join `paises` to `emissao_g_efeito_estufa` (on country ID and year).
4. It must join `paises` to `consumo_anual` (on country ID and year).
5. It must join `consumo_anual` to `fonte_energia` to get the names of the energy sources.

**The "Fan-out" and Pivot Problem:**
Because `consumo_anual` stores energy sources as individual rows (e.g., Country A has 5 rows for 2022 representing Coal, Solar, Wind, etc.), the database has to perform an expensive runtime `GROUP BY` and `PIVOT` operation to display this data side-by-side. 

**Denormalization Candidates:**
We need to eliminate the joins on `id_paises` and `ano` (year) by pre-joining these metrics. We also need to collapse the `continente` lookup and pivot the `fonte_energia` rows into columns.

---

### 2. Denormalization Design

To optimize for read latency, we will transition from a 3NF model to a **Pre-joined Wide Table (Snapshot Fact Table)**. 

*   **Grain:** One row per Country per Year. This is the exact level of granularity your business users query.
*   **Collapsing Relations (Redundant Attributes):** We will store `nome_pais` and `nome_continente` directly in the new table. This eliminates the need to join the `paises` and `continente` dimension tables for filtering or grouping.
*   **Precomputed Aggregates (Derived Attributes):** We will calculate `gee_per_capita` (`quantidade_emitida` / `quantidade_hab`) during the ETL/Write phase. This saves the CPU from doing division on millions of rows during `SELECT` queries.
*   **Pivoting (Row-to-Column):** We will flatten the `consumo_anual` and `fonte_energia` tables. Instead of multiple rows per year for energy types, we will create explicit columns (`perc_fonte_carvao`, `perc_fonte_solar`, etc.).
*   **Partitioning:** We will use **Table Partitioning by Range on `ano` (Year)**. Analytical queries almost always filter by a specific year or a range of years. Partition pruning will allow the database to skip scanning irrelevant years entirely.
*   **Index Strategy:** 
    *   A composite index on `(continente, ano)` to support fast regional time-series dashboards.
    *   A composite index on `(ano, gee_per_capita DESC)` to instantly serve "Top Polluters per Year" queries without requiring a runtime sort.
