### Executive Summary

As a principal database architect, I have reviewed your normalized schema and your proposed denormalized draft. Your intuition to use **Collapsing Relations** and **Adding Redundant Attributes** is correct for read-heavy analytical workloads. However, your proposed `fato_aluno_trajetoria` combines `matricula_ifb` (1:N from student) and `vinculo_empregaticio` (1:N from student) into a single flat table. **This creates a severe business correctness risk:** joining two independent 1:N relationships directly results in a Cartesian product (e.g., a student with 2 enrollments and 3 jobs will generate 6 rows, artificially inflating salary and enrollment counts during aggregations). 

To guarantee measurable read performance while strictly preserving business correctness, I have refined your design into a **Star Schema** utilizing PostgreSQL **Materialized Views**. We will implement one wide dimension for schools (`dim_escola_indicadores_anuais`) and **two distinct fact tables** (`fato_aluno_matricula` and `fato_aluno_emprego`). This approach eliminates join penalties at query time, prevents data duplication fan-outs, and allows safe, concurrent, and observable refreshes without impacting the transactional source-of-truth.

### 2. Denormalization Patterns Chosen

1.  **Wide Read Tables (Pre-joined Dimensions):** `dim_escola_indicadores_anuais`.
    *   *Justification:* School metrics (ENEM, indicators) are strictly annual. Pre-joining them by `(co_escola, nu_ano)` reduces a complex 5-table snowflake into a single sequential scan.
2.  **Fact-Dimension Split (Star Schema):** `fato_aluno_matricula` and `fato_aluno_emprego`.
    *   *Justification:* Resolves the Cartesian product risk. We embed the student demographic dimension directly into both fact tables (Wide Facts) because student attributes (`raca`, `sexo`, `idade`) are highly queried filters. 
3.  **Materialized Views for "Current State":** 
    *   *Justification:* Educational data (Censo, ENEM) and Employment data (RAIS) are typically updated in large annual or semesterly batches. Real-time CDC is unnecessary overhead. Materialized views allow atomic `CONCURRENTLY` refreshes, ensuring zero downtime for readers.

### 3. Proposed Denormalized Schema (Physical Design & DDL)

*Note: We use `MATERIALIZED VIEW` to encapsulate the ETL logic. Unique indexes are strictly required to allow `REFRESH MATERIALIZED VIEW CONCURRENTLY`.*

```sql
BEGIN;

-- ============================================================================
-- 1. WIDE DIMENSION: School Indicators (Grain: School + Year)
-- ============================================================================
CREATE MATERIALIZED VIEW public.dim_escola_indicadores_anuais AS
SELECT 
    COALESCE(i.co_escola_educacenso, d.co_escola_educacenso, p.co_escola_educacenso) AS co_escola_educacenso,
    COALESCE(i.nu_ano, d.nu_ano, p.nu_ano) AS nu_ano,
    e.no_escola_educacenso,
    e.tp_dependencia_adm_escola,
    e.tp_localizacao_escola,
    e.inse AS inse_escola,
    l.uf AS uf_escola,
    l.municipio AS municipio_escola,
    i.nu_taxa_permanencia,
    i.nu_taxa_aprovacao,
    i.nu_taxa_reprovacao,
    i.nu_taxa_abandono,
    i.pc_formacao_docente,
    i.porte_escola,
    p.nu_matriculas AS nu_matriculas_enem,
    p.nu_participantes_nec_esp AS nu_participantes_nec_esp_enem,
    p.nu_participantes AS nu_participantes_enem,
    p.nu_taxa_participacao AS nu_taxa_participacao_enem,
    d.nu_media_cn AS nu_media_cn_enem,
    d.nu_media_ch AS nu_media_ch_enem,
    d.nu_media_lp AS nu_media_lp_enem,
    d.nu_media_mt AS nu_media_mt_enem,
    d.nu_media_red AS nu_media_red_enem
FROM public.indicadores_escolares i
FULL OUTER JOIN public.desempenho_enem d 
    ON i.co_escola_educacenso = d.co_escola_educacenso AND i.nu_ano = d.nu_ano
FULL OUTER JOIN public.participacao_enem p 
    ON COALESCE(i.co_escola_educacenso, d.co_escola_educacenso) = p.co_escola_educacenso 
   AND COALESCE(i.nu_ano, d.nu_ano) = p.nu_ano
LEFT JOIN public.escola e 
    ON COALESCE(i.co_escola_educacenso, d.co_escola_educacenso, p.co_escola_educacenso) = e.co_escola_educacenso
LEFT JOIN public.localizacao l 
    ON e.id_loc = l.id_loc;

-- Indexing for concurrent refresh and fast filtering
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX uk_dim_escola_ano ON public.dim_escola_indicadores_anuais (co_escola_educacenso, nu_ano);
CREATE INDEX idx_dim_escola_uf ON public.dim_escola_indicadores_anuais (uf_escola);

-- ============================================================================
-- 2. FACT: Student Enrollments (Grain: 1 row per id_matricula)
-- ============================================================================
CREATE MATERIALIZED VIEW public.fato_aluno_matricula AS
SELECT 
    m.id_matricula,
    a.id_aluno,
    a.idade,
    a.sexo,
    a.raca,
    a.portador_deficiencia,
    m.ano AS ano_entrada_ifb,
    m.co_ciclo_matricula,
    m.periodo,
    m.situacao_matricula,
    m.tipo_cota,
    m.atestado_baixarenda,
    m.unidade_ensino,
    c.co_curso,
    c.no_curso,
    c.tipo_curso,
    c.ds_tipo_nivel,
    c.ds_eixo_tecnologico,
    c.modalidade_ensino,
    m.co_escola_educacenso
FROM public.matricula_ifb m
LEFT JOIN public.aluno_ifb a ON m.id_aluno = a.id_aluno
LEFT JOIN public.curso_ifb c ON m.co_curso = c.co_curso;

-- Indexing
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX uk_fato_matricula ON public.fato_aluno_matricula (id_matricula);
CREATE INDEX idx_fato_mat_curso ON public.fato_aluno_matricula (co_curso);
CREATE INDEX idx_fato_mat_escola ON public.fato_aluno_matricula (co_escola_educacenso);

-- ============================================================================
-- 3. FACT: Student Employment (Grain: 1 row per id_vinculo)
-- ============================================================================
CREATE MATERIALIZED VIEW public.fato_aluno_emprego AS
SELECT 
    v.id_vinculo,
    a.id_aluno,
    a.idade,
    a.sexo,
    a.raca,
    v.ano_rais,
    v.razao_social,
    v.tipo_vinculo_empregaticio,
    v.data_admissao_declarada,
    v.tempo_emprego,
    v.motivo_desligamento,
    v.indicador_vinculo_ativo,
    r.vl_remun_media_nom,
    r.vl_remun_media_sm,
    r.vl_remun_dezembro_nom,
    r.qtd_hora_contr,
    l.uf AS uf_vinculo,
    l.municipio AS municipio_vinculo
FROM public.vinculo_empregaticio v
LEFT JOIN public.aluno_ifb a ON v.id_aluno = a.id_aluno
LEFT JOIN public.remuneracao r ON v.id_remuneracao = r.id_remuneracao
LEFT JOIN public.localizacao l ON v.id_loc = l.id_loc;

-- Indexing
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX uk_fato_emprego ON public.fato_aluno_emprego (id_vinculo);
CREATE INDEX idx_fato_emp_ano ON public.fato_aluno_emprego (ano_rais);
CREATE INDEX idx_fato_emp_aluno ON public.fato_aluno_emprego (id_aluno);

COMMIT;
```

### 4. Consistency Architecture & Implementation Plan

**Data Lineage:**
*   Source tables remain the strict system of record.
*   The Materialized Views act as a read-only presentation layer. 

**Refresh Strategy (The "Safe, Observable, Reversible" Plan):**
1.  **Staleness Window:** Explicitly defined as **24 hours**. Educational data (ENEM, Censo) and RAIS data are inherently historical. Real-time reads are not a business requirement here.
2.  **Execution:** A nightly cron job or orchestrator (e.g., Airflow/pg_cron) executes:
    `REFRESH MATERIALIZED VIEW CONCURRENTLY public.dim_escola_indicadores_anuais;`
    *(Repeat for the two fact tables).*
3.  **Reversibility:** Because we are using Materialized Views, the normalized tables are completely untouched. If the denormalized layer fails or produces anomalies, BI tools can be temporarily repointed to the normalized views. To rollback entirely, simply `DROP MATERIALIZED VIEW`.

### 5. Risk Analysis & Operational Complexity

*   **Risk 1: The "String Join" Fragility (Explicit Assumption).** 
    *   *Issue:* You noted that `unidade_ensino` maps to `no_escola_educacenso` (a string-to-string relationship). String matching is highly vulnerable to typos and encoding issues.
    *   *Mitigation:* I intentionally left `co_escola_educacenso` in `fato_aluno_matricula`. Analysts should join `fato_aluno_matricula.co_escola_educacenso` to `dim_escola_indicadores_anuais.co_escola_educacenso` instead of relying on the text name.
*   **Risk 2: Storage Growth & Write Amplification.**
    *   *Issue:* Denormalization duplicates data (e.g., student demographics are repeated for every enrollment and job).
    *   *Mitigation:* Storage is cheap, but memory is not. By splitting into two fact tables, we avoided the exponential Cartesian product growth. The `CONCURRENTLY` refresh requires temporary disk space (approx. 2x the view size during the transaction), so ensure `work_mem` and disk space are provisioned for the nightly batch window.
*   **Monitoring/Alerts Required:**
    *   **Freshness Alert:** Monitor the `pg_stat_user_tables.last_autovacuum` or a custom logging table to ensure the nightly refresh completes before 06:00 AM.
    *   **Reconciliation Diff:** Run a daily validation query: `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM fato_aluno_matricula` vs `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM matricula_ifb`. They must be exactly equal. If not, an alert should fire indicating a dropped record (usually caused by an inner join creeping into the ETL; hence, I used `LEFT JOIN` and `FULL OUTER JOIN` strictly).
